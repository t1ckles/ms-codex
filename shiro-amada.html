Background

Shiro Amada's record begins with his presence at Side 2 during the Principality of Zeon's poison-gas attack on the colony there, an event commonly cited within this archive's sourcing as his motivation for a military career. Little else from his pre-service life is on file; what survives of his record begins almost entirely at the point he enters Federation officer training and receives transfer orders to Earth to take command of the Kojima Battalion's 08th Mobile Suit Team in Southeast Asia — a posting widely regarded within the wider Federation as a backwater assignment, far from the war's headline engagements.

That posting did not begin quietly. En route to Southeast Asia, Shiro intercepted a distress call from a damaged Federation GM and launched in a stopgap RB-79K Ball Type K to investigate, there being no proper mobile suit aboard his transport. He destroyed the attacking Zeon prototype and rescued the GM's pilot, Terry Sanders Jr., though his Ball was critically damaged in the process. Escaping the wreck, Shiro tracked the surviving Zeon pilot to a sunken Magellan-class hull and, after a close-quarters struggle, discovered the pilot was a woman — Aina Sahalin. Neither knew at the time that they would meet again, repeatedly, on opposite sides of the same regional campaign. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Distress Call in the Debris Field," for the full account.

Service Record

Taking Command

As commander of the 08th MS Team, Shiro was initially regarded by his more experienced pilots — particularly Karen Joshua — as inexperienced and reckless, a judgment not entirely without basis given his unconventional rescue of Sanders just before arriving at his post. Early jungle patrols tested that judgment directly, including the team's first contact with Zeon ace Norris Packard, and it was only over a string of subsequent engagements that Shiro began to earn the team's trust.

The Apsalus Engagements

Shiro's command tenure is defined almost entirely by the 08th Team's running engagement with Zeon's Apsalus Project. He led the team through its first encounter with the unarmed Apsalus I, and later commanded the high-risk intercept of the armed Apsalus II — an operation in which his Gundam Ground Type was destroyed clinging to the mobile armor's hull as it fled into the Himalayas during a snowstorm. Stranded for days afterward with the Apsalus II's pilot, Aina Sahalin, Shiro's already-complicated history with her deepened into something neither the Federation nor Zeon's command structure would have sanctioned. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Apsaras Program: First Contact" and "Shivering Mountain: Apsalus II Intercept."

Under Suspicion

When Shiro's connection to Aina Sahalin came to light, he was investigated and court-martialed by the Federation on suspicion of espionage — the subject of a separate compiled record, Miller's Report. Though acquitted, lingering distrust within the Kojima Battalion followed him for the rest of the campaign; both Karen Joshua and Terry Sanders Jr. were secretly issued standing orders by Capt. Isan Ryer to execute their own commander should he attempt to desert, unaware the other had received the same order. Cross-reference Operation Records, "The Espionage Inquiry."

The Mountain Stronghold

At the final siege of Zeon's mountain stronghold, Shiro deserted his post to reach Aina before Capt. Ryer's forces could kill her — an act for which Ryer ordered his summary execution if captured. Piloting the rebuilt Gundam Ez8, Shiro fought alongside Aina to bring down the completed Apsalus III, ending Ginias Sahalin's strike on the Federation position and effectively terminating the Apsalus Project at its source. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Siege of the Mountain Stronghold," for the complete engagement log.

Personal Notes

Both Shiro and the Ez8 went down with the destroyed Apsalus III into the stronghold's volcanic caldera and were logged as missing, presumed killed. Unconfirmed post-war reports, gathered independently by guerrilla liaison Kiki Rosita and support crewman Michel Ninorich, place a man matching Shiro's description living with Aina Sahalin in a remote mountain cabin, having deserted his commission; this archive treats the matter as unresolved pending further declassification.

Personnel who served alongside him consistently describe a commander whose early recklessness gave way to a stubborn, almost single-minded loyalty — to his pilots, and eventually to Aina specifically, even at direct cost to his own career and standing within the Federation chain of command.
