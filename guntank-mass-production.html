Overview

The Guntank Mass Production Type is a simplified, cost-reduced descendant of the original RX-75-4 Guntank prototype developed under the Federation's Project V program. Unlike a conventional bipedal mobile suit, it rides on caterpillar treads rather than legs and has no manipulator hands — by most working definitions it sits closer to a self-propelled artillery piece than a true mobile suit, a fact reflected in its purely fire-support role rather than front-line combat duty.

The mass-production redesign dropped the original Guntank's expensive Core Block escape system in exchange for a torso that can independently rotate to track targets — addressing a major shortcoming of the prototype, which had to reposition its entire chassis to aim. Cannon mounts were also standardized to a universal pattern, letting maintenance crews swap in different weapon or equipment loads depending on mission profile.

Operational History

Production of the Mass Production Guntank was limited, with only a handful of units fielded across the entire Federation army, mostly in base-defense or long-range artillery roles. Its single most consequential deployment came with the Kojima Battalion in Southeast Asia: three units were committed to bombard Zeon's hidden mountain stronghold during the campaign's final assault, intended to prevent any mass evacuation by shuttle. Col. Norris Packard, fighting alone in his Gouf Custom to protect that same evacuation route, specifically targeted and destroyed all three Guntanks despite the 08th MS Team's efforts to stop him. Their loss removed the Federation's heavy artillery cover at the critical moment, allowing Zeon to safely launch the completed Apsalus III. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Siege of the Mountain Stronghold," and the Gouf Custom unit dossier for the engagement from the opposing side.
