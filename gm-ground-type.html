Overview

The GM Ground Type is the mass-production backbone of the Kojima Battalion's mobile suit complement — a cheaper, simpler cousin to the RX-79[G] Gundam Ground Type, built in far greater numbers and crewed by pilots without the elite training reserved for Gundam-rated officers. Where the Gundam Ground Type represents a small, semi-custom production run built from leftover RX-78 data, the GM Ground Type is the genuine workhorse of Federation ground forces in this theater, and its operational data collection contributed directly to later Federation mobile suit development.

Its head design — a simplified, single-eye-camera silhouette distinct from the RX-79's binocular sensor array — would go on to become the visual template for the entire RGM-79 GM line that defined Federation mass-production mobile suits for the remainder of the war and beyond.

Operational History

Deployed to Southeast Asia on a priority basis to gather front-line combat data, the GM Ground Type formed the numerical core of the Kojima Battalion's ground forces alongside the smaller RX-79[G] complement fielded by the 08th MS Team specifically. Squadrons of GM Ground Types fought throughout the campaign's running engagements with Zeon ground forces, and select units were field-modified into the long-range GM Sniper configuration for fire-support duty. GM Ground Types were also present in the war's final major ground offensive, Operation Odessa.
