Background

Eledore Massis is the longest-tenured member of the 08th MS Team, having served with the unit longer than any of its mobile suit pilots, including those who preceded Shiro Amada's arrival. Rather than piloting a Gundam himself, he crews the team's support hovertruck, providing radar coverage and battlefield intelligence from the rear rather than fighting directly on the front line — a role that, while less glamorous than a mobile suit posting, made him an effectively indispensable fixture of the unit's day-to-day operational capability.

Service Record

Sensor Support Across the Campaign

By his own admission, Eledore does not consider himself cut out to be a soldier, and he is documented as openly anxious under fire — a trait several of his fellow crew members noted candidly in surviving accounts. Despite this, his sensor work from the hovertruck was a consistent fixture of the 08th Team's field operations throughout the Southeast Asia campaign, including the unit's running engagements against the Apsalus Project. His radar coverage and early-warning calls are referenced, directly or indirectly, across nearly every major operation in this archive's holdings for the 08th Team.

Under Fire

Eledore has repeatedly put himself in danger when the team needed him to, particularly where Karen Joshua's safety was concerned — a pattern noted often enough in surviving accounts to suggest a personal stake well beyond standard unit loyalty. He was wounded badly enough on at least one occasion to require direct field treatment, stabilized by Karen herself using her prior medical training.

Off-Duty Conduct

Off duty, Eledore pursued a sideline interest in music, a pursuit that put him at odds with Karen's stricter sense of discipline more than once. Surviving accounts treat this tension affectionately rather than as genuine conflict — a recurring, low-stakes friction point within an otherwise close-knit crew.

Personal Notes

Eledore survived the war alongside the rest of the surviving 08th Team roster. His working partnership with Michel Ninorich, who joined the hovertruck crew alongside Shiro early in the campaign, is consistently documented across the unit's record as one of the most stable and reliable working relationships within the entire team — the two functioning, in practice, as a single coordinated support unit for the duration of the campaign.
