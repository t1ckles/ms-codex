Overview

The Gouf line is Zeonic Company's dedicated answer to close-quarters combat, built around the doctrine that not every engagement should be decided at rifle range. Where the mass-produced Zaku II is built as a generalist — cheap, simple to maintain, available in overwhelming numbers — the Gouf trades some of that production efficiency for a heavier melee-weapons loadout and a pilot-skill ceiling that rewards an experienced operator far more than it does sheer numbers.

The Custom variant catalogued here is an ace-tuned refinement of the standard MS-07B airframe. Where the line-production Gouf relies on a simple finger-mounted vulcan for ranged suppression, the Custom replaces it with a true five-fingered manipulator hand and adds a detachable triple-barrel 35mm Gatling gun at the forearm. Archive cataloguing of this specific airframe is built almost entirely from after-action accounts of a single engagement: its use by Col. Norris Packard, a Zeon ace attached to the Sahalin family's security detail, during the defense of a supply withdrawal route in the closing weeks of the Southeast Asia campaign. No other confirmed Gouf Custom sorties from this theater are on file.

Pilot Pairing

Packard is recorded flying two distinct mobile suit types across the campaign: an earlier MS-06JC Zaku II Type C, used in his first recorded contact with the 08th MS Team, and the Gouf Custom catalogued here, used exclusively in his final engagement. The shift from a mass-production Zaku to a specialist close-combat custom mirrors his shift from a conventional defensive posture to the deliberate, all-or-nothing last stand that defined his death.

  • Heat SaberMelee, thermal-edged — primary weapon in the duel against the Ez8
  • Heat WireWhip-type weapon doubling as an unconventional mobility tool, used for both attack and unexpected flanking maneuvers

Tactical Notes

The combination of detachable Gatling weapons and a heat wire gave the Gouf Custom an unusually flexible engagement profile for a Zeon close-combat unit: capable of opening at range with sustained automatic fire, discarding spent ordnance to commit to melee, and using the heat wire to close distance or reposition around an opponent's flank rather than relying on raw thruster output. Against the 08th MS Team's more numerous but individually less experienced Gundam Ground Type pilots, this flexibility allowed a single unit to dictate the terms of an engagement that should, on paper, have been hopelessly one-sided.

Operational History

Packard's only confirmed deployment of the Gouf Custom came during a single-handed defense of a city block, intended to secure a withdrawal corridor for the Zeon hospital transport Kerguelen. Facing down a portion of the 08th MS Team protecting a column of artillery Guntanks, Packard's Custom disabled two Gundam Ground Type units and destroyed multiple Guntanks in rapid succession, using the Gatling shield and forearm gun to suppress at range before closing with the heat saber. Federation accounts of the engagement describe repeated heat-saber strikes against Shiro Amada's Gundam Ez8 that dented its arm armor without penetrating — a near-miss margin this archive's sourcing credits as much to the Ez8's reinforced rebuild as to any failure on Packard's part.

The engagement ended in Packard's death in direct combat with Shiro Amada's Ez8, but not before he completed his stated objective of eliminating the Guntank artillery threatening the Kerguelen's escape route. That sacrifice was rendered moot when Capt. Isan Ryer ordered the hospital transport destroyed regardless. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Siege of the Mountain Stronghold," for the complete engagement log.
