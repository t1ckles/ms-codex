Strategic Context

Operation Record 05 covers the most complex and personally transformative operational period of the entire Southeast Asia campaign. Its three episodes span a tactical ambush against an armed version of the weapon the team had only previously encountered in prototype form, the most dramatic unit loss in the 08th Team's service record, an extended high-altitude survival incident that was not an operation in any formal sense but that shaped every event that followed, and a concluding chapter that is simultaneously a clean military victory — the Apsalus II was denied to the Federation, but destroyed rather than captured — and the operational precondition for every catastrophe in the final operation.

The strategic setup came directly from Shiro's court-martial bargain: locate the Apsalus base through a deep-penetration mission and his name would be cleared. That mission was episode 9. What happened in episodes 10 and 11 was not the mission anyone planned.

Episode 9: "Front Line"

The Reconnaissance Assault

With Federation command now aware that a strategic Zeon weapon program was operating somewhere in the Southeast Asia theater — and Ryer framing the Apsalus as a threat sufficient to justify committing 08th Team resources to finding it — the team launched a deep reconnaissance push into Zeon-held territory, tasked with locating the hidden development base. Jet Core Boosters were used for rapid aerial insertion beyond the team's normal operational range.

The reconnaissance produced the base's approximate location — mountain sector, fortified — but also confirmed what the 08th Team had suspected from their desert engagements: the Apsalus II was significantly more capable than the I. Sensor data from Karen Joshua's unit, caught in a Gaw attack during the reconnaissance push, confirmed the armed weapon system's output range. Shiro pulled Karen's unit out during the Gaw engagement rather than continuing the push, a decision that protected the team's pilot strength at the cost of some additional intelligence collection. Command accepted the tradeoff.

Episodes 10–11: "The Shuddering Mountain, Parts 1 & 2"

The Ambush Setup

Acting on the reconnaissance intelligence, the 08th MS Team established an ambush position in the desert sector, calculated to intercept the Apsalus II during a subsequent test flight. The mission objective was capture if possible, destruction if not — command wanted the technology intact, and had specifically equipped the team with capture-oriented loadouts. What they found when the Apsalus II appeared was a weapon firing a six-emitter multi-target mega particle cannon array that none of their combat simulations had adequately modeled.

The Pursuit and the Fall

The Apsalus II did not submit to the ambush. Aina Sahalin, piloting it, identified the threat, fired the cannon system to scatter the ground team, and broke for altitude and escape. The rest of the engagement is documented as one of the most consequential single decisions in the campaign's record: Shiro Amada latched his Gundam Ground Type (unit 081) onto the hull of the fleeing Apsalus II rather than allowing it to escape.

Aina told him, on open comms, that this would kill them both. She wasn't wrong about the physics. The Apsalus II's attempt to escape with a Gundam Ground Type hanging off its hull sent the combined weight into an uncontrolled descent during a snowstorm over the Himalayan mountain range. Unit 081 was destroyed in the fall — the impact and subsequent crash scattered the wreckage across a mountainside. Shiro survived. Aina survived. They landed separately in an uninhabitable environment with no communications to either side.

The Mountain — Days Together

What followed for several days on that mountain is not formally documented in any Federation after-action report because no Federation officer was present to observe it, and Shiro's own account of the period is sparse in operational terms. What the archive can state, drawing on his subsequent testimony and on Aina Sahalin's later confirmed choices: the two of them cooperated to survive — sharing warmth, treating injuries, occupying the same space without weapons drawn for the first time under circumstances where they had time to actually talk. When Federation rescue forces arrived and Aina was faced with capture, she destroyed the Apsalus II remotely rather than allow it to fall into Federation hands. She then slipped away before she could be taken into custody.

The Apsalus II was denied to the Federation. Shiro was recovered. His Gundam Ground Type was destroyed. The campaign's only strategic gain from this operation was the confirmed denial of the Apsalus II and the approximate base location the reconnaissance had produced in episode 9. Against those gains: one destroyed Gundam, and a personal connection between the 08th Team's commanding officer and the Apsalus Project's test pilot that had now deepened from a complicated prior encounter to something Federation command would have had serious difficulty characterizing in any operational document without immediately reopening the espionage inquiry.

Outcome & After-Action Assessment

Unit 081 was rebuilt from available salvage as the Gundam Ez8, a field-modified successor designated "extra zero-eight" — the team's unofficial extra unit. The rebuild was completed in time for the campaign's final operation. On the Zeon side, the destruction of the Apsalus II accelerated Ginias Sahalin's already-deteriorating timeline: with the project having lost its second prototype, he doubled down on completing the Apsalus III regardless of Zeon high command's subsequent orders to halt the program. The mountain incident did not end the Apsalus Project. It ended the project's window for proceeding with any operational sanity. Everything Ginias did from this point forward was in defiance of standing orders and conducted with the judgment of a man who had staked everything on a single outcome and could not accept that the window for achieving it was closing.
