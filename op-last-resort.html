Strategic Context

The assault on Zeon's mountain stronghold was the culmination of the entire Southeast Asia campaign — the Federation's full-force attempt to eliminate the Apsalus Project at source before Ginias Sahalin could complete the Apsalus III and launch an unauthorized strike against Jaburo. Ryer's plan committed resources from beyond the Kojima Battalion: Jet Core Boosters for bombing runs to reshape the mountain itself, the 07th MS Team assigned to tunnel infiltration of the base interior, and the 08th MS Team as escort for an artillery element of three Guntank Mass Production Types tasked with bombarding the mountain's access routes to prevent any mass Zeon evacuation.

What the plan did not adequately account for was Norris Packard.

The Packard Engagement: Defense of the Withdrawal Corridor

One Man, One Mission

While the broader Federation assault was still deploying, Norris Packard launched alone in his MS-07B-3 Gouf Custom into a bombed-out urban area between the Federation assault force and the Zeon mountain base. His objective was not to hold the base — Zeon command had already ordered evacuation, and Ginias Sahalin, in open defiance of those orders, had sealed the base against its own people rather than comply. Norris's objective was to secure a withdrawal corridor long enough for the Zeon hospital transport Kerguelen — carrying wounded Zeon personnel — to escape safely.

To do that, he had to destroy the three Guntank Mass Production Types that were screening the 08th Team's advance and whose artillery would have pinned the Kerguelen on any escape route through the area. He faced them while the 08th MS Team's Gundam units tried to protect them. He disabled two Gundam Ground Types in rapid succession, using his Gouf Custom's Gatling shield and forearm gun to suppress at range and its heat saber to close for melee, then methodically picked apart the Guntank column despite the best efforts of the remaining Federation pilots to stop him.

The Duel with Shiro

What finally stopped Norris was Shiro Amada, returning in the field-rebuilt Gundam Ez8. The duel between them was one of the most technically demanding in the theater's record: Norris's experience, reflexes, and Gouf Custom's close-combat optimization versus the Ez8's reinforced rebuild and Shiro's reckless refusal to stop attacking even after the heat saber strikes had dented the Ez8's arm armor to the point of structural compromise. Surviving accounts credit Norris with recognizing, somewhere during the exchange, that he was fighting the man Aina Sahalin loved — not something that stopped him, but something that changed the texture of how he chose to end the engagement.

Norris was killed. The Ez8 was heavily damaged but mobile. The last Guntank was destroyed by Norris in his final moments — he had completed his objective before he died. The Kerguelen's escape corridor was clear. None of it mattered. Capt. Isan Ryer ordered the Kerguelen destroyed regardless, citing the ongoing attack rather than honoring any ceasefire implication. The hospital transport was shot out of the sky with wounded Zeon personnel aboard.

Ginias Sahalin's Final Hours

Collapse Inside the Base

While the battle for the approach corridor played out outside, Ginias Sahalin was completing the Apsalus III inside the sealed mountain base over the bodies of everything his project had cost him. He had already killed Zeon officer Yuri Kellerne when Kellerne tried to override the base lockdown and allow the evacuation he'd been ordered to conduct. He had executed his own development team rather than allow the project to be abandoned with the work uncatalogued. He assembled his senior staff for a toast to mark the Apsalus III's completion, apparently unaware of or indifferent to the Federation assault consuming the mountain above him.

Aina Sahalin, who had come to the base to reach Norris and found him already gone, was present when Ginias launched the Apsalus III in an unauthorized strike against the Federation besieging forces. She attempted to stand down the attack from inside the cockpit. Ginias shot her directly. She survived because a pocket watch carried in her coat absorbed the round — a detail that appears in multiple independent accounts of the incident and is treated in this archive as confirmed fact rather than dramatic embellishment.

The Apsalus III and the Final Engagement

The Apsalus III fired its mega particle cannon into the Federation's forward command position — confirming both its operational status and the threat the entire Southeast Asia campaign had been built around. Shiro Amada deserted his post to reach Aina, an act for which Ryer formally ordered his summary execution on capture. He found her. Karen Joshua, having acquired a weapons lock on Shiro's deserting Gundam Ez8, deliberately fired wide rather than carry out Ryer's order.

In the Gundam Ez8's final sortie, Shiro and Aina fought the Apsalus III together — Aina controlling one of the Ez8's arms manually, Shiro at the controls. The mobile armor was brought down into the mountain base's volcanic caldera. The Ez8 went down with it. Ginias Sahalin was killed in the destruction. Aina and Shiro were logged as missing, presumed killed.

Epilogue: U.C. 0080

Disbandment and the Search

The One Year War ended with the Armistice of January U.C. 0080. The 08th MS Team was formally disbanded as part of peacetime drawdown. Karen Joshua and Terry Sanders Jr. are confirmed as surviving the war. Eledore Massis survived. Michel Ninorich, discharged from Federation service, departed with Kiki Rosita on a private search for Shiro Amada — neither willing to accept the MIA/presumed dead designation as the final record.

Their search eventually brought them, via a group of children living in the jungle, to a remote mountain cabin in the former operational area. Inside: a man matching Shiro Amada's description, and a woman matching Aina Sahalin's, visibly pregnant. The children had been looking after them since the battle.

This archive's treatment of that account is consistent with its treatment throughout: unconfirmed, pending further declassification, but present in the record because it is present in the sourcing and because the sourcing is the record. Whether Shiro Amada and Aina Sahalin survived the fall of the Apsalus III is a question this archive cannot answer with certainty. What it can say is that two people who had fought the same campaign from opposite sides, had survived two separate near-fatal crashes together, and had each made the choice to end their war on their own terms rather than anyone else's — that those two people represent, more than any of the equipment or engagements catalogued in the rest of this archive's holdings, what the Southeast Asia campaign was actually about when you strip the tactical notation away.

Outcome & After-Action Assessment

The Apsalus Project was terminated. Zeon's mountain stronghold was taken. The Federation's Southeast Asia campaign concluded with its stated objectives met. The cost: multiple Federation units destroyed, three Guntank crews killed by Norris Packard in an engagement he won on his own terms, the Kerguelen and its wounded destroyed by Capt. Ryer in a decision that produced the campaign's single most unambiguous atrocity, and two of the campaign's central figures — Shiro Amada and Aina Sahalin — officially missing and presumed dead. Ginias Sahalin was killed. Norris Packard was killed. The battle they all fought produced, in the end, a clean Federation military victory and a human balance sheet that does not resolve cleanly into any category the archive has a field for.
