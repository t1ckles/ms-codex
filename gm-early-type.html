Overview

The GM Early Type is the pre-production ancestor of the entire RGM-79 GM lineage — developed in parallel with the ground-pounding RX-79[G] Gundam Ground Type as the Federation scrambled to field a mass-production answer to Zeon's mobile suit advantage. Designed for space combat and closely based on the RX-78-2 Gundam, the Early Type incorporates several cost-driven compromises that distinguish it from later, more refined GM variants: titanium alloy armor rather than the Gundam's Luna Titanium, and a simpler, reliability-focused armament loadout suited to rapid mass production rather than peak individual performance.

Operational History

This archive's primary connection to the GM Early Type is biographical rather than tactical: Terry Sanders Jr.'s pre-transfer record places him as a Federation Space Forces test pilot flying this class prior to the disaster that cost him his entire prior team and led to his reassignment into the 08th MS Team. The Early Type's space-combat baseline made it unsuited to the demands of the Southeast Asia jungle theater, and the type was effectively superseded in this theater by the purpose-built GM Ground Type.
