Background

A veteran Earth Federation Space Forces test pilot prior to his Southeast Asia posting, Sanders was the sole survivor of a brutal engagement against an experimental Zeon MS-06RD-4 Zaku High Mobility Test Type and its escort. Every other member of his prior team was killed in that action; Sanders himself was pinned down, out of ammunition, until Shiro Amada — flying a stopgap RB-79K Ball Type K — intervened and destroyed the Zeon prototype, allowing Sanders to retreat to a waiting transport. With his team gone, Sanders was reassigned to the Kojima Battalion's 08th MS Team, placed under the direct command of the man who had just saved his life. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Distress Call in the Debris Field."

The disaster that brought Sanders to the 08th Team was, by his own account, not an isolated incident. He carried with him a reputation that had followed him through several prior postings, one that would shape his early service with his new unit far more than his evident skill as a heavy-weapons pilot.

Service Record

The Reaper's Reputation

Among Federation ranks, Sanders carried the nickname "Sanders the Reaper" — a grim shorthand for a pattern that had defined a string of prior postings, each of which was annihilated on its third mission together, leaving him as the sole survivor each time. Whether genuine bad luck or simple statistical coincidence, the reputation followed him into the 08th Team, and harassment from pilots in other units took a visible toll on his confidence. Shortly before the 08th Team's own third joint mission together, Sanders attempted to resign his post rather than risk his new teammates' lives on what he believed was an inevitable repeat. Shiro refused the request outright.

Breaking the Pattern

On that third mission, the 08th Team encountered the Apsalus I mobile armor for the first time during its forced descent into Federation territory. All pilots involved, including Sanders, came through the engagement damaged but alive — breaking, by his own account, the cycle that had haunted his service record up to that point. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Apsaras Program: First Contact." From that engagement forward, Sanders served as one of the team's most reliable pilots and, by wide consensus among surviving accounts, Shiro Amada's single most personally loyal subordinate.

The Espionage Inquiry

During the period of suspicion surrounding Shiro's relationship with Aina Sahalin, Sanders was secretly issued the same standing order given to Karen Joshua: execute Shiro if he attempted to desert. Neither pilot was aware the other carried an identical order. Sanders ultimately did not act on it, even as Shiro broke from the unit during the final siege of Zeon's mountain stronghold. Cross-reference Operation Records, "The Espionage Inquiry."

Personal Notes

A serious, reserved man who carried his survivor's guilt quietly rather than openly, Sanders is documented across surviving accounts as the most steadfastly loyal of the 08th Team's pilots to Shiro Amada specifically — a loyalty traceable directly to the circumstances of his transfer into the unit. He survived the war as one of the 08th Team's confirmed living veterans.
