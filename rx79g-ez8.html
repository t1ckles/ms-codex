Overview

The Ez8 was not a planned production model — it was what was left of Shiro Amada's original Gundam Ground Type after the unit was nearly destroyed clinging to the hull of the fleeing Apsalus II mobile armor during the team's mountain intercept operation. With dedicated RX-79 spare parts essentially nonexistent in the Southeast Asia theater, the 08th MS Team's maintenance crew rebuilt the suit using whatever stock was on hand, including components scavenged from captured Zeon hardware. The result was reclassified rather than simply repaired: the Ez8, short for "extra zero-eight," referencing its status as the team's unofficial extra unit, distinct from the battalion's standard-issue 081–083 production run.

The name carries a deliberate historical echo as well. "Ez8" mirrors the M4A3E8(76)W "Easy Eight" variant of the World War II-era M4 Sherman medium tank — a vehicle that, like this Gundam, received its defining upgrades not at the factory but in the field, through ad hoc modification driven by frontline experience rather than a formal procurement cycle. Archive cataloguing treats this naming choice as intentional commentary on the unit's origins.

Modification Scope

Despite the extensive rebuild, baseline mobility and combat performance carried over essentially unchanged from the Ground Type — this was a repair shaped by necessity, not a redesign chasing improved specifications. Where it does measurably differ is in weight (reduced, owing to parts substitutions), field communications range, and a modest gain in overall responsiveness, all attributable more to the rebuild's lighter materials mix than to any deliberate performance program.

Airframe & Armor Changes

Chest Armor

The most visible change on the Ez8 is its chest plating, fabricated from a pair of salvaged Zaku II shoulder shields welded together. The resulting plate is hinged to flip open for maintenance access and is rated to withstand a direct hit from a Zaku II's 175mm recoilless rifle with only superficial scarring — a substantial, if improvised, upgrade over the Ground Type's original chest armor.

Head Sensor Mast

The standard Gundam V-fin antenna array was replaced with a single fixed communications antenna. This was partly a parts-availability decision and partly a tactical one: protruding dual-blade antennae were a recognized liability in dense jungle terrain, prone to snagging and accidental damage. The Ez8 is consequently one of the few Gundam-type units in Federation service without the signature V-fin silhouette.

Fixed Armament Layout

Where the Ground Type mounted head-top sensor equipment and a chest-mounted multi-launcher, the Ez8's rebuild dropped the multi-launcher entirely as non-essential and relocated point-defense weaponry: twin 35mm machine guns in the head, on a mount with wide traverse, and a separate 12.7mm anti-personnel gun on a turret at the base of the chest, fed from dual drum magazines with roughly 60° of horizontal and +20°/-60° of vertical travel.

Operational History

Despite its scavenged origins, the Ez8 proved equal to, and in some respects better than, the Ground Type it replaced. Shiro Amada flew it for the remainder of the Southeast Asia campaign, including continued patrol and escort duty through the campaign's final weeks.

Its best-documented engagement is the close-quarters city battle against Col. Norris Packard's Gouf Custom, fought while the Ez8 screened a column of artillery Guntanks bound for the assault on Zeon's mountain stronghold. Packard's Custom proved a severe test of the rebuild — heat saber strikes dented the Ez8's arm armor repeatedly without penetrating, a fact this archive's sourcing attributes as much to the airframe's reinforced rebuild as to Shiro's piloting. The duel ended in Packard's death and the Ez8 intact, clearing the way for the final assault on the stronghold. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Siege of the Mountain Stronghold," for the complete engagement log.

The Ez8's final sortie came in the climactic battle against the completed Apsalus III. The unit did not survive the engagement: heavily damaged, it went down into the stronghold's volcanic caldera together with the destroyed mobile armor. Both occupants aboard at the time — Shiro Amada piloting, and Aina Sahalin, who is on record as having manipulated one of the unit's arms during the joint effort to bring down the Apsalus III — were presumed dead. Wreckage was never recovered for archive cataloguing.
