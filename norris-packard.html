Background

Norris Packard served the aristocratic Sahalin family for decades, raising Ginias and Aina Sahalin from childhood after their parents' deaths. Aina in particular regarded him as a surrogate father; by her own account he closely resembled her late father in manner. Trained as a mobile suit pilot when the One Year War began, Packard was entrusted with the overall defense of Ginias Sahalin's secret base in Southeast Asia, balancing his loyalty to the family with obedience to Ginias's increasingly erratic command as the war's later stages wore on.

Service Record

First Contact with the 08th MS Team

Packard's first recorded encounter with the 08th MS Team came while piloting a standard MS-06JC Zaku II Type C; though a capable pilot, he was forced to retreat against Shiro Amada's superior numbers and the technological edge of the Gundam Ground Type. Cross-reference Operation Records, "First Contact: Southeast Asia Front."

Air Support for the Apsalus Tests

Packard later flew air support in a Dopp aircraft during one of Aina Sahalin's Apsalus test flights, retreating only after his aircraft was damaged and Aina was left stranded — a decision surviving accounts treat not as cowardice but as a hard tactical call under fire, consistent with his otherwise unwavering loyalty to the Sahalin siblings throughout the rest of the campaign.

Final Stand

Packard's defining engagement came in the campaign's final weeks. Going out alone in a Gouf Custom to secure a safe withdrawal corridor for the hospital transport Kerguelen, he encountered a detachment of the 08th MS Team guarding a column of artillery Guntanks. Packard disabled two Gundam Ground Type units and destroyed several of the Guntanks before confronting the team's commander, Shiro Amada, in the rebuilt Gundam Ez8. Despite superior skill and experience, Packard was killed in the resulting duel — though not before completing his objective of eliminating the artillery threat. His sacrifice was rendered moot when the Kerguelen was destroyed regardless, by Federation order. Cross-reference Operation Records, "Siege of the Mountain Stronghold," for the complete engagement log, and the Gouf Custom unit dossier for full technical detail on the duel itself.

Personal Notes

Packard's stern, intimidating bearing masked a deeply loyal and honorable character. As he came to understand the bond between Aina and Shiro Amada, his view of the Federation officer shifted from simple enemy to a measure of Shiro's worthiness for Aina — a judgment his final battle is widely read, within this archive's sourcing, as having ultimately rendered in Shiro's favor, even in defeat.
