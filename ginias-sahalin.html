Background

Ginias Sahalin was a Zeon technical officer and the architect of the Apsalus Project, a Zeon initiative to build a mobile armor capable of destroying the Earth Federation's subterranean Jaburo headquarters with a single decisive strike. Ginias suffered from an undisclosed chronic illness from childhood, requiring ongoing medication; this condition worsened steadily over the course of the war, with an increasingly corrosive effect on his judgment and mental stability as the project's timeline dragged on without resolution.

Service Record

Directing the Apsalus Program

As director of the Apsalus Project, Ginias oversaw all three stages of mobile armor development, from the unarmed flight-test Apsalus I through to the fully weaponized Apsalus III, with his sister Aina serving throughout as lead test pilot. The project carried Zeon high command's funding priority following the failure of Operation British to neutralize Jaburo outright, placing considerable institutional weight — and, by extension, considerable personal pressure on Ginias — behind its success. Cross-reference the Apsalus Project's unit dossier for full technical and developmental detail.

Defiance of Standing Orders

Following the collapse of Zeon's position in Operation Odessa, Zeon command ordered the project halted in favor of evacuating remaining forces off-world. Ginias did not comply, continuing development of the Apsalus III in secret rather than abandon a program he had staked his career, and increasingly his own stability, on completing.

Collapse

In the project's final days, Ginias's deteriorating state escalated into open violence against his own side: he sealed the base's evacuation routes, killing Zeon officer Yuri Kellerne in the process when Kellerne attempted to override the lockdown to assist the evacuation, and ultimately executed his own development team rather than allow the unfinished project to be abandoned or its secrets to leave the base. He launched the completed Apsalus III in an unauthorized strike against the Federation forces besieging the base, piloting it alongside Aina.

Personal Notes

When Aina turned against the attack and sided with Shiro Amada, Ginias shot his own sister rather than stand down; she survived only because a pocket watch in her coat absorbed the round. Ginias was killed when the Apsalus III was destroyed in the resulting battle, though not before firing a final shot from the mobile armor's main weapon that struck the Federation's forward command position. Surviving accounts treat his final actions less as calculated military strategy than as the terminal stage of a long personal unraveling — a project that began as an attempt to restore his family's standing ending instead in the deaths of nearly everyone connected to it, including, by his own hand, almost his sister.
