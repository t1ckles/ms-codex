Overview

The Zaku I is the Principality of Zeon's first true mass-produced mobile suit, predating and ultimately superseded by the more capable Zaku II line. By the time of the Southeast Asia campaign, surviving Zaku I units in front-line service are mostly flown by veteran pilots who came up through the type early in the war and, in some cases, simply prefer the airframe they're most experienced on over a formal reissue to newer equipment — a pattern that produced a small number of well-maintained, individually customized Zaku I units alongside the type's broader retirement to reserve and second-line duty.

Operational History

A commander-custom Zaku I piloted by an officer identified in surviving records only as Topp was part of a small three-suit Zeon detachment that occupied a guerrilla-aligned village during the 08th MS Team's early operations in the theater, alongside two J-type Zaku II commander units. Neither side in that engagement sought the resulting bloodshed, but the encounter ended in heavy losses on both sides regardless. Cross-reference Operation Records, "First Contact: Southeast Asia Front."
