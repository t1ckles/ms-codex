Strategic Context

On 6 October U.C. 0079, Ensign Shiro Amada was aboard a Federation transport en route to Earth to assume command of the Kojima Battalion's 08th Mobile Suit Team in Southeast Asia. The transport carried no proper mobile suit complement — a routine logistics reality for officer transfers that were not classified as combat deployments. This detail would define everything about what followed.

Also aboard the same or an adjacent vessel — accounts are inconsistent on this specific point — was Chief Petty Officer Terry Sanders Jr., sole survivor of an earlier test-team action against a Zeon prototype. Sanders was in transit under reassignment orders with his prior unit destroyed. The two men had not yet met.

Stated Objective

None at the outset. This engagement was initiated entirely on Shiro Amada's personal decision, not under any order from Kojima Battalion command or from the transport's own captain. Upon intercepting a distress call from a damaged Federation GM, Shiro launched without authorization in the only mobile weapon available: a stopgap RB-79K Ball Type K — an early-production mobile pod not designed for combat, crewed by a pilot with no prior experience of the type.

Engagement Summary

The Rescue

Sanders' GM Early Type had been ambushed by a Zeon MS-06RD-4 Zaku High Mobility Test Type and its escort, losing its entire accompanying team in the engagement. By the time Shiro located the contact, Sanders was out of ammunition, pinned down, and moments from destruction. Shiro's Ball Type K had two fixed-angle 180mm recoilless cannons and a rocket-guided cable winch — and no combat manual for using either of them against a Zeon prototype with a significant mobility and armor advantage.

What Shiro did instead of a straight engagement was improvise: he used a large piece of debris as concealment, drifting toward the Zaku unseen until he was close enough for the winch to matter. He fired the Ball's rocket-propelled grappling cable, snaring the Zaku's arm, then used the cable to swing the pod around the enemy unit in a wide arc — entangling the Zaku's limb and bringing the Ball's fixed cannons into point-blank range of the Zaku's head unit. When the Zaku pilot drew a heat hawk to cut the cable and destroy the Ball at close range, Shiro held the blade away from his cockpit with the Ball's manipulator arm just long enough to fire. The Zaku's head assembly was destroyed at contact range. Both machines were critically damaged in the exchange; the Ball began breaking apart almost immediately. Shiro evacuated just before it detonated. Sanders was recovered.

Post-Action: Contact with Zeon Pilot

The Zaku's pilot also survived. Shiro tracked the contact's life-sign to the hull of a sunken Magellan-class Federation battleship drifting in the debris field, and followed. A close-quarters standoff developed inside the wreck — two pilots from opposite sides, both armed with sidearms, neither willing to fire first at close range in the confined interior of a dead warship. When the standoff finally broke, Shiro physically subdued the Zeon pilot — and discovered, pulling back the suit's visor, that he was holding a young woman.

No identification was made at the time. Neither pilot had any basis for recognizing the other as a combatant on the same regional front. The woman — later confirmed as Aina Sahalin, lead test pilot for Zeon's Apsalus Project — and Shiro parted without further conflict. The encounter lasted perhaps twenty minutes by surviving account estimates. Neither the Federation transport's crew nor Sanders, recovering separately, had any record of this conversation occurring.

Outcome & After-Action Assessment

CPO Sanders was recovered. The MS-06RD-4 Zaku prototype was confirmed destroyed, a genuine intelligence gain given the type's experimental status. The Ball Type K was lost. Shiro sustained no significant injury.

On paper, this is a successful rescue at the cost of a single expendable mobile pod. In operational terms, it produced something far harder to quantify: the first meeting, under lethal circumstances, between the commanding officer of the Kojima Battalion's 08th MS Team and the test pilot who would fly every iteration of the strategic weapon the battalion would spend the rest of the Southeast Asia campaign trying to destroy. Neither knew what the other was. Both would remember the encounter. This archive's assessment of the entire subsequent campaign in this theater is that no subsequent engagement can be fully understood without this one as its antecedent.
